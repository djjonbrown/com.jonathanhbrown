Care Management Mobile
2017
For a large integrated health delivery company that was building a new Care Management platform, I was tasked with designing a solution to support Case Managers working both on and offline while performing assessments of Long-Term Services & Support Members.

Case Managers Observations and Interviews

In order to comprehend the end-users’ day-to-day routine, demands of the job and HIPAA compliance, we interviewed and observed Case Managers for whom this application is being developed.



Over the course of two adjacent weeks, we travelled the length and breadth of Delaware to observe Case Managers performing their tasks and to interview them regarding their goals and motivations. We observed in (3) Nursing Facilities from Wilmington to Delaware City, and in (5) member’s homes from Long Neck to Smyrna to Bear.

‍During our visit, we interviewed (9) Case Managers about the training provided, tech support availability and being HIPAA compliant. One of the observations was an initial assessment of member at their home which took about 2 hours. Most of the quarterly assessments were completed in under an hour.
‍

Insights from Observations

The entire project team collaborated in effort to understand and prioritize the current processes and issues.




Each Case Manager follows their own path through the Workbook.



We expected to observe Case Managers walking through the Assessment from start to finish. While we did witness Case Managers using the Workbook to drive the agenda of the Assessment Meeting, we saw more Case Managers jumping around the Assessment based on the context of their conversation. The Case Managers tried to document their nonlinear conversation in the workbook - frequently were forced to quickly jot their notes on paper, because the narrative was not available at times to capture the conversation. It is our understanding this breaks HIPAA requirements.



Case Managers do not trust the Workbook’s ‘copy’ feature.



A significant portion of the Case Managers we observed chose to rebuild the workbook from scratch every time rather than using the Workbook’s copy feature. Each Case Manager provided observers with a story about how the feature would copy irrelevant data forward, but would not copy very relevant data. Those who used the copy feature spent a significant amount of time validating that it had copied appropriate data forward.

Despite the feedback, there were some Case Managers who loved the copy feature and relied heavily upon it. Also, a Case Manager who did not use the copy feature recognized that a copy feature they could trust would cut the time the spent using Workbook in half.



Questionnaires are not tailored to the type of residence the member lives in.



Some questions the Case Manager asked the Member were irrelevant and were not geared to the facility the Member lived.



Manual data validation is time consuming.



A lot of time is spent confirming the data is accurate before the Case Manager uploads the workbook. After the assessment, the Case Manager’s main focus is to ensure the many date fields are not empty and match, because theService Center will flag the workbook as potential fraud if the dates do not match. This manual date validation provides no value to Highmark and is tedious for the Case Manager.Also, any validations that do not pass the Service Center’s review process requires a tedious back and forth process between the Case Manager and Service Center to ensure theWorkbook is updated appropriately.



Case manager and member struggle with signature functionality.



Member has difficulty signing the workbook using the touchscreen. Sometimes, the Case Manager repeatedly requests the member to sign because the member touches outside the signature box.



Current process requires lots of double documentation.



The Case Manager must upload the same Workbook to theScheduler and Onbase. In addition, they must ensure thedate, time and narrative of the visit is captured Care Connect.This creates additional work for the Case Manager as well ascreates potential compliance issues.



Case Managers click Save all the time.



In order to avoid losing work, the Case Managers click the save button obsessively. They all shared horror stories of lost data and lost signatures. We observed one case manager reopen the completed assessment before she left the member’s home. In the past, she has arrived home to find the signatures missing after she collected them. Now, she ensures they were saved before she leaves.

Principles

In order to build software that fully meets the needs of our customer the principles will be our foundation. The principles will be the building blocks for our product and will inform both the design and functionality. A successful product is one that incorporates all the principles.




Support the Conversation



CM Mobile is meant to document the conversation between two (or three) human beings. Human conversation is not linear and doesn’t follow a single path. The software needs to allow the Case Manager to seamlessly jump between data elements where she is collecting data, but provide guidance on where to go next if she gets stuck.

Design icon Jacob Neilsen says that there should be a match between system and the real world- “The system should speak the users’ language, with words, phrases and concepts familiar to the user, rather than system-oriented terms. Follow real-world conventions, making information appear in a natural and logical order.”

Summary questions, like “has anything changed with your diagnoses?” should be asked in order to determine if a full battery of questions needs to be asked.

The narrative should always be readily available for note taking



Optimize for Intermediates



Case Managers using CM Mobile will be using it for a longtime (see observation). The interface must provide advanced features and shortcuts that improve the efficiency of CaseManagers as they become more familiar with the software.Case Managers will find the tools in their working set are front and center, easy to find and remember. We will minimize the cognitive load on the user by making objects, actions, and options visible and easily retrievable.Beginners will be onboarded with real-world training and apprenticeship.



Transparency and Feedback build trust



The Case Managers don’t trust the workbook. When it takes action on their behalf, it changes things they don’t understand and don’t notice.

CM Mobile will provide visual feedback of all actions taken on behalf of the user. Particularly, in future, when the copy feature is fully implemented, fields that are completed on the user’s behalf will give the user visual clues.

When errors occur, CM Mobile will express the error message in plain English, and constructively suggest solutions to the errors.



Tedious features will be automated



When Case Managers are using they workbook, they often struggle and/or spend extraordinary amounts of time dealing with basic features (saving, copying, etc) that should be fundamental to the applications design and purpose. For example, rather than expecting users to choose when to save, we will assume they want to save everything and allow them to undo when they accidentally go too far. By incorporating these features into the core of the application, Case Manager’s will become more efficient as well as have a great user experience.



Seamlessly integrate online and offline



By ensuring a seamless integration between the offline and online Care Management applications, CM Mobile will reduce the Case Manager’s workload and ensure Case Managers are always in compliance.

Approach



To better understand the context surrounding the project, the SDLC Project team facilitated a session in the offices of Highmark Delaware. The team consisted of three members, the Technical Lead, the Business Analyst, and the Product Designer. These roles would balance the viability, feasibility, and desirability of the solution as it takes shape.

The following are the objectives that the new CM Mobile application is expected to meet:

  • Fulfill compliance requirements for serving the DelawareMedicaid LTSS Member population
  • Provide a great user experience with seamless integrationto the online Care Management Platform
  • Build a laptop deployable version of the CM Mobile application that is extensible to other device platforms in the future
  • Establish a configurable and extensible architecture to support future business goals and objectives (i.e. MN Elderly Waiver)
