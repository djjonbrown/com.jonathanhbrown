Care Management Mobile
2017
For a large integrated health delivery company that was building a new Care Management platform, I was tasked with designing a solution to support Case Managers working both on and offline while performing assessments of Long-Term Support & Services Members.

Throughline

Long-Term Services & Support are a variety of services that help people with disabilities or chronic health conditions live independently. LTSS can include home care, community-based services, or care in facilities like nursing homes. Medicaid is the primary payer. A case manager regularly visits the patient to ensure they receive all entitled services and that caretakers receive the appropriate compensation. They capture their visit notes in an application they called "the workbook."
As we observed, we saw the case managers writing on slips of paper the notes that should have been captured in the workbook. The workflow of that application had been codified as if the case manager would walk through each section of a survey asking exactly the prescribed questions in the prescribed order.
The case manager had been assigned to the case for many years, and the company had remarkably low turn over. We noticed the rapport the case managers had built over many meetings with the same patients. They were having a conversation, not following a script. When they heard an answer to a question that was on a subsequent screen, the case managers would frantically write on a piece of paper and enter the information into the workbook later. This approach was not only inefficient, it also broke HIPPA guidelines.

Approach



To better understand the context surrounding the project, the SDLC Project team facilitated a session in the offices of Highmark Delaware. The team consisted of three members, the Technical Lead, the Business Analyst, and the Product Designer. These roles would balance the viability, feasibility, and desirability of the solution as it takes shape.

The following are the objectives that the new CM Mobile application is expected to meet:

  • Fulfill compliance requirements for serving the DelawareMedicaid LTSS Member population
  • Provide a great user experience with seamless integrationto the online Care Management Platform
  • Build a laptop deployable version of the CM Mobile application that is extensible to other device platforms in the future
  • Establish a configurable and extensible architecture to support future business goals and objectives (i.e. MN Elderly Waiver)

In order to comprehend the end-users’ day-to-day routine, demands of the job and HIPAA compliance, we interviewed and observed Case Managers for whom this application is being developed.

Over the course of two adjacent weeks, SDLC travelled the length and breadth of Delaware to observe Case Managers performing their tasks and to interview them regarding their goals and motivations. We observed in (3) Nursing Facilities from Wilmington to Delaware City, and in (6) member’s homes from Long Neck to Smyrna to Bear.
